Research Process

Project Type

UHP Signature Work — Independent Research Paper

1. Project Overview & Origins

This project began with a question that emerged from my involvement in an international arbitration panel event held during California International Arbitration Week in March 2026.

The panel, co-organized with Professor Marcus S. Quintanilla of the UC Davis School of Law, examined how trade secret disputes are handled in international arbitration proceedings. I wrote the original script for the panel presentation, and the experience made me want to explore the underlying legal questions more rigorously.

2. Preparation & Literature Review

I began by identifying the central tension I wanted to examine: the paradox that arbitration's confidentiality, which makes it attractive for trade secret holders, simultaneously constrains their ability to gather the evidence needed to prove misappropriation.

I reviewed primary legal materials including the Federal Arbitration Act, the IBA Rules on the Taking of Evidence, the New York Convention, and AAA Employment Rules, as well as secondary sources and case law on vacatur proceedings.

3. Research & Writing Structure

I structured the paper around eight sections, moving from the mechanics of how trade secret claims enter arbitration, through a comparative analysis of litigation and arbitration evidentiary standards, to an original four-part evidence strategy framework.

The paper draws on a hypothetical scenario involving a technology startup and a departing co-founder, which I developed for the original panel presentation and refined for this paper.

4. Presentation & Ultimate Impact

The research was initially presented in panel format at California International Arbitration Week in March 2026, before being developed into this full-length academic paper.

This project has deepened my understanding of international dispute resolution and confirmed my intention to pursue a legal career in this field. It has also given me practical experience conducting legal research, constructing legal arguments, and writing in the style expected of legal scholarship.

The Evidentiary Core Paradox

Trade secret holders choose international arbitration primarily because of its confidential nature, preventing intellectual property exposure to market competitors. However, this private venue limits the tribunal's compulsive power to order broad discovery, creating a critical hurdle when a claimant must prove secret misappropriation by bad-faith adversaries.

Academic Reflection

What I Would Keep the Same

The Power of Concrete Hypotheticals

I would keep the decision to ground the paper in a concrete hypothetical scenario. Having a running factual situation to return to throughout the analysis made the legal arguments more accessible and gave the paper a narrative thread that I think strengthened it considerably.

What I Would Change

Deeper Empirical Scope

If I were starting over, I would begin the research phase earlier and allocate more time to the case law analysis sections. The vacatur case law in particular is a rich area that I was only able to survey at a high level, and a more comprehensive empirical analysis would have strengthened the paper's evidentiary claims.

UHP Project Recommendation

Would I Recommend this Project?

Yes, without hesitation, particularly for students who are considering law school or a career in a field adjacent to law. The process of conducting independent legal research, constructing an argument across twenty pages, and presenting it to an audience of practitioners forced me to develop skills that no coursework had previously demanded of me. The difficulty was real, but so was the reward.

Undergrad Impact

Influence on Personal Development

This project confirmed for me that legal scholarship is something I want to pursue seriously. It gave me a writing sample I am genuinely proud of, a deeper understanding of international arbitration as a field, and a clearer sense of the kind of lawyer I want to become. It also taught me that the most interesting legal questions are often the ones that sit at the intersection of competing values, and that the most honest scholarship acknowledges that tension rather than pretending to resolve it.
